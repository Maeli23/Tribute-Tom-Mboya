Tribute to Tom Mboya

"A visionary leader and architect of modern Kenya"

Tom Joseph Mboya (1930-1969) was a prominent kenyan trade unionist, educationist, Pan-Africanist, and statesman. He played a vital role in Kenya's struggle for independence and later in shaping the nations economic and social development.

Key Achievements

  • One of the youngest and most influential leaders in Kenya's independence movement.
  • Organized the"Airlift Africa" program, which sent hundreds of East African students to study in the United States
  • Served as Minister for Justice and Constitutional Affairs, and later as Minister for Economic Planning and Development.
  • Instrumental in drafting policies that laid the foundation for Kenya's development
"We must treat our people as a resource to be developed, not as a burden to be managed."
